Spinach Feta Quiche

Quiche is the perfect picnic food, it's easy to transport and great hot or cold. This spinach and feta quiche has a rich, buttery pastry that encases a fluffy, creamy filling of spinach and feta cheese.

Spinach Feta Quiche

10 oz frozen chopped spinach, thawed
3/4 block feta cheese
1 pie crust, chilled
6 large eggs
3/4 cup milk
3/4 tsp kosher salt
1/4 tsp black pepper
3/4 tsp kosher salt
1/4 tsp nutmeg
1/4 tsp hot sauce, such as Tobasco

  1. Preheat the oven to 375F/196C.
  2. Prepare spinach, cooking long enough to remove bitterness. Squeeze out as much liquid as possible and set aside.
  3. Line crust in dish with aluminum foil. Fill with pie weights or dried beans, and if still chilled, bake for 10 minutes on a baking sheet. If not, refrigerate for 10 mins, then blind bake.
  4. Remove beans and foil, brush with thin egg wash, then return the crust to the oven and bake until light golden, about 3 minutes more. Reduce the oven temperature to 350F / 177C.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, whip the eggs ~3min, then add in milk, salt, pepper, nutmeg, and Tabasco.
  6. Scatter spinach evenly over the bottom of the blind-baked crust. Sprinkle the feta over the top of the spinach. Carefully pour the egg mixture over the top.
  7. Bake the quiche on the baking sheet, until a knife inserted 1 inch from the edge of the crust comes out clean and the center is set, about 40 to 50 minutes (or longer). If at any point the crust begins to brown more than you would like, protect it with foil or a pie crust shield. Let rest 5 to 10 minutes.
